Information sent through unsecured electronic channels may not be protected by attorney-client privilege and may be subject to disclosure under applicable law.

Professional Standards of Confidentiality

The legal profession in India is governed by strict standards of confidentiality and professional conduct. These include:

  • Protection of client confidences and secrets
  • Maintenance of attorney-client privilege
  • Ethical handling of sensitive information
  • Compliance with professional conduct rules prescribed by the Bar Council of India
  • Adherence to the Advocates Act, 1961 and related regulations

These standards apply to all established lawyer-client relationships and are fundamental to the practice of law.
